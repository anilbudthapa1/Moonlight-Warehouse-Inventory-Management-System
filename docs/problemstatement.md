# Problem Statement

Moonlight is an Australian e-commerce grocery business that depends on efficient warehouse operations to maintain accurate stock levels, manage perishable goods, and support timely order fulfilment. In a grocery environment, warehouse processes are more complex than in general retail because products often have batch numbers, expiry dates, storage location requirements, and high turnover rates. If warehouse operations are handled through manual processes, disconnected spreadsheets, or poorly integrated systems, the business can experience stock inaccuracies, delays in stock updates, poor visibility of available inventory, increased product wastage, and slower order preparation. These issues reduce operational efficiency, affect customer satisfaction, and limit the organisation’s ability to scale in a competitive market. For Moonlight, there is a clear need for a centralized warehouse management system that improves inventory control, reduces manual effort, and supports better business decision-making.

## Key Problems Identified

- Inaccurate inventory records caused by manual data entry and fragmented processes  
- Limited real-time visibility of stock levels, product movement, and warehouse status  
- Difficulty tracking batch numbers and expiry dates for perishable grocery items  
- Increased risk of stockouts, overstocking, and product wastage  
- Inefficient stock-in, stock-out, and stock adjustment processes  
- Delays in warehouse-related order preparation and dispatch support  
- Lack of centralized control over products, suppliers, locations, and inventory data  
- Reduced ability of managers and staff to make timely and informed operational decisions  

## Affected Users and Stakeholders

- Warehouse staff responsible for receiving, storing, and issuing stock  
- Warehouse managers who need accurate inventory visibility and control  
- Business owners and administrators who rely on operational reports and performance data  
- Suppliers whose products and deliveries must be properly tracked  
- Customers who may be indirectly affected by stock unavailability, delays, or order inaccuracies  

## Root Causes of the Problem

- Dependence on manual or paper-based warehouse processes  
- Use of disconnected spreadsheets or non-integrated tools  
- Absence of an automated and centralized inventory system  
- Lack of expiry-sensitive stock monitoring for grocery products  
- Growing operational complexity as the business expands  
- Insufficient reporting and dashboard visibility for management  

## Why This Problem Matters in Australia

- Australian e-commerce customers expect fast, accurate, and reliable service  
- Grocery businesses must manage waste carefully due to perishability and cost pressure  
- Businesses need stronger digital systems to remain competitive in the Australian market  
- Efficient warehouse operations are essential for supporting service quality and growth  
- Poor stock control can directly affect profitability, compliance, and customer trust  

## Project Need

To address these challenges, Moonlight requires a modern web-based warehouse management system that can:

- Provide secure, role-based access for system users  
- Centralize management of products, suppliers, warehouse locations, and inventory  
- Track stock movement accurately in real time  
- Support batch and expiry date monitoring  
- Generate alerts for low-stock and near-expiry items  
- Improve reporting, visibility, and operational decision-making  
- Reduce manual work and improve warehouse efficiency  

## Conclusion

In conclusion, the main problem faced by Moonlight is the lack of an efficient and centralized warehouse inventory management system for its e-commerce grocery operations. Current manual and fragmented processes create inaccuracies, wastage, delays, and reduced operational visibility. Because the business operates in a competitive Australian grocery market, solving this problem is important for improving efficiency, reducing risk, supporting customer satisfaction, and enabling future growth. The proposed Moonlight Warehouse Management System is intended to address these operational issues by delivering a secure, scalable, and user-friendly digital solution for warehouse inventory management.
